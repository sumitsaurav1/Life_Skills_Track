# What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening? 
Active listening is an essential skill that involves fully engaging with the speaker, understanding their message, and responding appropriately.
##1. Give Your Full Attention:
Focus solely on the speaker and eliminate distractions. Put away your phone, avoid interrupting, and maintain eye contact to show that you are fully present and interested in what they are saying. 
##2. Listen without Judgment: 
Suspend judgment and avoid forming opinions prematurely. Open yourself to the speaker's perspective, even if it differs from your own. Be empathetic and try to see things from their point of view.
## 3. Show Verbal and Non-Verbal Cues:
 Use verbal cues like nodding, verbal affirmations (e.g., "I see," "I understand"), and brief encouragements (e.g., "Go on," "Tell me more") to show that you are actively listening. Additionally, use appropriate facial expressions and body language to convey interest and understanding.
## 4. Provide Feedback and Summarize: 
After the speaker has finished sharing their thoughts, provide feedback to demonstrate that you were actively listening. 
## 5. Avoid Interrupting: 
 Allow the speaker to complete their thoughts before responding. Interrupting can be seen as disrespectful and may hinder the flow of information.
## 6. Ask Clarifying Questions: 
When the speaker pauses, ask clarifying questions to ensure you understand their message.
# According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?
## Active Engagement: 
listening requires active engagement, where you focus entirely on the speaker and avoid distractions.
## Non-Verbal Cues: 
Reflective listening involves using appropriate non-verbal cues, such as nodding, maintaining eye contact, and using facial expressions, to demonstrate your attentiveness and empathy.
## Verbal Reflection: 
Reflective listening includes paraphrasing or repeating back the speaker's words in your own words. By doing so, you confirm your understanding and show the speaker that you are truly listening to their message.
## Validation and Acknowledgment: 
When practicing reflective listening, you validate the speaker's feelings and emotions by acknowledging them.
# What are the obstacles in your listening process?
## Distractions: 
 External distractions like noise, interruptions, or a busy environment can make it challenging to focus on the speaker's message.
## Lack of Attention:
Not giving the speaker your full attention can result in missing important details or nuances in their message.
## Multitasking:
Trying to do other tasks while listening may reduce your ability to fully grasp the speaker's message.
## Assumption of Understanding: 
Assuming that you understand the speaker's message without seeking clarification can lead to misinterpretation.
# What can you do to improve your listening?
## Be Present and Mindful:
Practice being fully present in conversations. Avoid distractions, and give your undivided attention to the speaker.
## Maintain Eye Contact: 
Eye contact shows engagement and interest. It helps you focus on the speaker and signals that you are actively listening.
## Stay Open-Minded:
Avoid making judgments or assumptions prematurely. Be open to different perspectives and ideas.
## Be Patient:
Some speakers may take longer to express themselves or may need more time to find the right words. Be patient and allow them to share at their pace.
